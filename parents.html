Covering Rush, Pledging, and Life as a Phi Sig

Rushing and Pledging

Rush can be a daunting topic for parents. Likely, you have plenty of questions, thoughts, and possibly even personal experiences with the Greek rush process. During rush, your son will have the opportunity to meet many different men across Grounds, and search for a group where he feels comfortable. Likewise, the different fraternities your son visits will be getting to know him, and assessing how he will fit in to the existing social dynamics in the fraternity.

At the University of Virginia, this is a three round process where men are encouraged to attend social events with the Brothers of the fraternities they rush. Over the course of the three rounds, fraternities will invite men who they feel fit in well to subsequent rounds. At Phi Sigma Kappa, we look for young men who embody or could embody our three Cardinal Principles of Brotherhood, Scholarship, and Character. More complete information on the entire process can be found at the UVA IFC website, and the formal IFC regulations on rush can be found in this PDF.

The Pleding process is very different from Rush. During the Phi Sigma Kappa pledging process, Associate Members undergo a program wherein they learn the history of the fraternity and the standards they are now held to. In addition, the Associate Members are expected to get to know each active Brother. Phi Sigma Kappa does not condone or engage in hazing or hazing related activites.
