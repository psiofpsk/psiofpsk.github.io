Support the Ψ Chapter's Capital Campaign

We're raising money to support Fall and Spring Rush, host Alumni Events, and renovate 1702.

Why we have a Capital Campaign

Phi Sigma Kappa at the University of Virginia has had periodic moments of growth and decline throughout the 100+ years the chapter has been active. Over the past few semesters our membership numbers have fallen slightly, and our house hasn't seen any major repairs in nearly half a century.

The Capital Campaign, started in the Fall Semester of 2017 by Brother Gabriel Maalouf (SEAS 2018), has set the goal of raising money over 10 years for two reasons. Our goals are to generate enough capital to carry out meaningful repairs on the house to maintain habitability for futre generations of brothers, and to have enough funds available to carry out normal chapter operations for all active brothers, regardless of actual chapter size.

The first major success of the Capital Campaign occured just prior to the Spring 2018 semester, where alums generously donated over $5,000 to ensure that we had a successful Spring Rush. Thanks to the generosity of our alums, we were able to pledge 10 men to our Brotherhood.

Our next immediate goals for the Campaign are the replacement of the dated water heater in the house, estimated to cost between $7,500 and $10,000, and the continual success of Spring Rush to ensure the chapter remains active under new IFC regulation. Long term goals include the removal of asbestos from the house, and a full renovation of the inside. With the help of our Alumni, we believe these goals can be achieved.
